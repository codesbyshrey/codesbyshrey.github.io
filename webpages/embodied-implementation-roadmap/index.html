It takes an average of 17 years for evidence-based research to enter routine clinical practice. Implementation science has mapped organizational variables, but largely ignored the most critical interface: the embodied human at the moment of contact.

The Bottleneck of Memory

In any health encounter, we impose three types of load on a patient's finite working memory. Intrinsic Load is the unavoidable complexity of the medical task. Extraneous Load is the "noise" of bad design or chaotic environments. Germane Load is the effort spent building a mental model of the health behavior.

Currently displaying a standard, unoptimized intervention. High extraneous load causes decision noise and cognitive fatigue, preventing germane learning.

Systems Science: The Cognitive Light Cone

This final section scales the individual experience up to population-level systems science. Drawing on Michael Levin's TAME framework, the "Cognitive Light Cone" defines the spatiotemporal boundary of a system's goals. Structural adversity shrinks this cone. Hover over the data points to understand how environmental constraints limit an agent's capability horizon, explaining disparities in health intervention uptake.

The Translation Principle

We cannot ask a person with a "shrunk" light cone (focused on immediate survival due to structural inequity) to care about a 20-year preventative outcome. We must design implementations that expand the light cone by first providing stability and somatic safety. As observed in physical therapy, Implementation is translation—reframing solutions to match the user's existing architecture and cognitive horizon.
