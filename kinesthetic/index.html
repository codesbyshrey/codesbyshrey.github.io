Ecological Psychology

Kinesthetic Literacy treats action as organism-environment coupling: perception is active, affordances are body-scaled, and design should make useful action possibilities easier to perceive.

Applied Biomechanics

The framework draws on force, torque, gait, fascia, wearable sensors, and biotensegrity to explain how structure, feedback, and movement interact.

Cognitive Neuromechanics

Motor control, perception-action coupling, predictive models, and nervous-system regulation shape how people learn skills and recover confidence in movement.

Product Translation

The framework informs coaching tools, haptic interfaces, wellness intelligence products, and public systems writing under Practice Intuition.
