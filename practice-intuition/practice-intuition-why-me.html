A personal note

I've been where
you might be.

In late 2022, I developed thoracic outlet syndrome — presenting initially as ulnar and radial neuropathy — while simultaneously transitioning careers into software development. The irony: I would later work most closely at the Wellness Group with patients experiencing the exact same presentation.

I had to learn to retrain my own body, adapt my environment, and rebuild patterns from the ground up — while staying functional in a demanding new role. That experience gave me something no certification can: the knowledge of what it actually feels like to do this work on yourself.

"When you work with me, you're working with someone who's sat in the same chair — and found their way out of it."

Personal experience with neurological rehabilitation, ergonomic retraining, and occupational adaptation.
I resonated with my patients at the Wellness Group — and they resonated with me — because I'd lived a version of their story.
Patience, context, and empathy aren't soft extras — they're baked into how I work with every client.
